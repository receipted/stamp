# The Turn Primitive

## Definition

A turn is an attributed, sequenced, receipted move in an obligation space
that changes the space of valid next moves.

## Why Turn and Not Something Else

- An **event** is anything that happened.
- A **witness** is evidence that something happened.
- A **transform** is computation over input.
- A **receipt** is portable proof.
- A **turn** is the coordination move that can carry all of those — and be answered.

Without turns you can have logs, transforms, provenance, even receipts.
But you cannot have reciprocity. No dialogue, no delegation, no challenge,
no ratification, no accountability in sequence.

The turn is where a system stops emitting and starts coordinating.

## The Three-Phrase Security Model

- **"Take your turn"** — produce a receipted move. You're authorized.
- **"Wait for your turn"** — the chain determines sequence, not you.
- **"It's not your turn"** — the gate rejects unauthorized actors.

Authorization, sequencing, and attribution in three phrases a child understands.

## Six Dimensions of Turn

| Dimension | What "turn" means |
|---|---|
| Attribution | who acted |
| Sequencing | when in the chain |
| Authorization | whether they were allowed to |
| Transformation | raw → formed |
| Epistemic delta | what changed in the knowledge state |
| Cyclicality | the output feeds the next input |

No other word carries all six simultaneously.

## The Smell Test

Something is a turn if losing it would make you unable to reconstruct:
**who changed the next-step space for whom.**

## Four Orthogonal Axes

```
Mother type:  what kind of claim         (CONTRACT/CONSTRAINT/UNCERTAINTY/RELATION/WITNESS)
Turn act:     what kind of move          (ask/answer/propose/challenge/ratify/reject/handoff)
Binding tier: what authority it carries   (observed/proposed/ratified)
Custody:      how verifiable it is        (local/chained/merklized/zk_proved/anchored)
```

## Turn Acts (coordination vocabulary)

- ask
- answer
- propose
- witness
- challenge
- commit
- delegate
- ratify
- reject
- handoff

These are not mother types. Mother types classify what the turn carries.
Turn acts classify what coordination move the turn makes.

## Why "Turnchain" Is the Engine Name

Stamps are unilateral — you stamp something and it's done.
Turns are bilateral — someone has to answer.
The chain isn't just a sequence of proofs.
It's a sequence of moves in a coordination game
where each move changes what's possible next.
